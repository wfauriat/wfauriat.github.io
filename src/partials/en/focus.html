What I care about

As a researcher, over the past ten years, I've been particularly interested in methodological questions.

How do we make good predictions from data — and more importantly, how do we know how much to trust them? Is a conclusion robust, or could things change? Could we be wrong?

My expertise sits at the intersection of Machine Learning (ML), Uncertainty Quantification (UQ), and decision science: knowing how to build relevant models, but also knowing when they shouldn't be too confident.

What I want to do

Today, I want to turn my expertise into concrete deliverables — code, useful applications: data pipelines, predictive models, APIs, decision-support interfaces.

I've recently worked to gain a full-stack perspective on application development: I can contribute at every level of the software stack — data, models, back-end, front-end, deployment — ensuring that the whole system is coherent and makes effective use of available information.
