Value of Information in Maintenance

▾

Fauriat, W., & Zio, E. (2020). Optimization of an aperiodic sequential inspection and condition-based maintenance policy driven by value of information. Reliability Engineering & System Safety, 204, 107133.

Decision-theoretic framework for optimal maintenance scheduling using Value of Information (VoI). Addresses when and how to inspect degrading systems by quantifying the economic benefit of condition monitoring versus the cost of inspections and failures.

Key Contributions

  • VoI-driven framework for aperiodic inspection scheduling optimization
  • Bayesian updating of degradation models with sequential inspection data
  • Trade-off analysis between inspection costs, failure risks, and information gain
  • Application to condition-based maintenance in industrial systems

Estimation of road profile variability

▾

Fauriat, W., Mattrand, C., Gayton, N., Beakou, A., & Cembrzynski, T. (2016). Estimation of road profile variability from measured vehicle responses. Vehicle System Dynamics, 54(5), 585-605.

Data processing algorithm for estimating road profiles from vehicle dynamic responses. Addresses the inverse problem of inferring road excitation from measured vehicle displacement and acceleration using Kalman filtering in a stochastic framework.

Key Contributions

  • Kalman filtering-based algorithm for road profile reconstruction from vehicle sensors
  • Stochastic inverse problem formulation for uncertainty quantification
  • Validation on experimental vehicle dynamics measurements
  • Application to automotive durability assessment and road characterization

AK-SYS: System Reliability Method

▾

Fauriat, W., & Gayton, N. (2014). AK-SYS: an adaptation of the AK-MCS method for system reliability. Reliability Engineering & System Safety, 123, 137-144.

Novel adaptive Kriging method for efficient system reliability analysis. Extends the AK-MCS approach to handle series/parallel systems and complex failure configurations through intelligent sampling strategies.

Key Contributions

  • Adaptive enrichment strategy for Kriging surrogate models in system reliability
  • Efficient rare event probability estimation for series and parallel systems
  • Significant reduction in computational cost compared to Monte Carlo methods
  • Demonstrated on structural mechanics and engineering reliability problems

Key concepts
